# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are founders, experts, independent professionals, and small teams who need a landing page, corporate site, promo page, or interface with a clear task. They usually arrive in comparison mode and need to understand quickly whether this designer can bring structure, visual discipline, and a clean path to action.

Secondary users are developers, collaborators, or art-direction stakeholders who evaluate the site as evidence of handoff quality, system thinking, and frontend precision.

## Product Purpose

This project is a personal portfolio site for a UI/UX and web designer. Its job is to position the designer as someone who designs websites as systems: structure first, then visual foundation, then user flow, then final polish and handoff.

Success looks like this:

- the visitor understands the designer's positioning in the first screen
- the interface feels deliberate, calm, and structurally confident
- the work section reads as short case evidence, not as a decorative gallery
- the contact section makes it easy to start a project conversation with a clear task

## Brand Personality

Adult, precise, editorial.

The voice should feel self-assured, restrained, and sharply organized. It should not sound sales-heavy, over-friendly, or inflated. The interface should communicate rhythm, hierarchy, clear page logic, and visual control. Emotional goals: trust, clarity, composure, and premium discipline.

## Anti-references

- Generic agency landing pages with oversized marketing claims and shallow case tiles
- Loud SaaS dashboard aesthetics
- Neon gradients, dark glossy cards, and glassmorphism-heavy surfaces
- Noisy portfolio galleries that rely on motion instead of hierarchy
- Generic AI-looking sections that repeat the same card pattern without a point of view
- Over-decorated 3D scenes, blobs, particles, or unrelated illustration systems
- Empty copy about creativity without concrete process or structure

## Design Principles

- Lead with structure before decoration. The site should show how the designer thinks through page logic, hierarchy, and composition before it shows visual flourish.
- Present the website as a system. Sections should communicate a chain: visual foundation, user path, conversion logic, and handoff quality.
- Premium through control. Use calm contrast, visible structure, exact spacing, and disciplined typography instead of spectacle.
- Keep every section useful. The hero, works, and contact blocks should all move the visitor toward understanding or action.
- Motion must clarify, not perform. Animation should support selection, sequencing, and polish without turning into the subject.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Maintain readable contrast for all primary text, metadata, and interactive controls
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for non-essential animation
- Keep the message understandable even when motion is disabled
- Preserve visible focus states and predictable interaction patterns
- Favor readable line lengths, clear hierarchy, and comfortable tap targets across desktop and mobile
